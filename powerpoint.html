Microsoft PowerPoint

Microsoft PowerPoint is a presentation program. It is used by many people for presenting different things. Today, we're going to show you the basics of this application and how use it.

When you first load up Microsoft PowerPoint, you are presented with the home page. From here {image below) you can either start a new document (yellow), or open a document you might have been sent or have downloaded (red). Your recently opened documents will appear below (green).

The next thing to talk about is the tool bar (black). This is what will help you edit and change the look of your document to suit your style. Each section has its own list of options and is really nicely laid out for you.

Once you click on any of the given tools, a list of options will pop up. For example the home tool, which is basically a roundup of all the most important tools on word. This includes tools for Font, Paragraph, and Style. Once you click over any of these tools they will tell you there purpose and how to perform them.

Another tool is the design tab. This is for people who want insert tables into there documents or add a cover page or edit the page break. You can insert many things like charts, shapes and pictures.

Once you click on tables it will come up how many collumns and rows you want in your table, along with changes for the width.

The Design Tab will help you design your document the way you want it. You can change the layout of how the text is presented, or change the color of the page and even add a watermark.
