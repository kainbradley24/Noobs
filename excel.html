Microsoft Excel

Microsoft Excel is a spreadsheet developed by Microsoft for Windows. It is an data spreadsheet application and is used by many different people for different reasons. Today, we're going to show you the basics of this application and how use it.

When you first load up Microsoft Excel, you are presented with the home page. From here {image below) you can either start a new document (yellow), or open a document you might have been sent or have downloaded (red). Your recently opened documents will appear below (blue).

The next thing to talk about is the tool bar (black). This is what will help you edit and change the look of your spreadsheet to suit your needs. Each section has its own list of options and is really nicely laid out for you. You can see on the page (yellow) that the spreadsheet is divided into little sections rows and collumns.

Once you click on any of the given tools, a list of options will pop up. For example the insert tab. Tools like the Tables and Charts allow you to add and edit your perfect spreadsheet. Once you click over any of these tools they will tell you there purpose and how to perform them.

Another tool is the page layout tab. This is for designing the look of your spreadsheet. It comes with many different options to make your spreadsheet look the way you want it to look.

The Formulas tab is used to insert functions, define the name, create the name range, and review the formula. Once you make a formula, it will apply to the given data on your spreadsheet.

The Data tab is used to import lage types of data from the web. The imported data will be slotted into your spreadsheet.
