Microsoft Word

Okay let's get started. Microsoft Word is a word processor published by Microsoft. It is an office productivity application and is used by many different people for different reasons. Today, we're going to show you the basics of this application and how use it.

When you first load up PowerPoint, you are presented with the home page. From here {image below) you can either start a new document (yellow), or open a document you might have been sent or have downloaded (blue). Your recently opened documents will appear below (green).

The next thing to talk about is the tool bar (black). This is what will help you edit and change the look of your presentation to suit your style. Each section has its own list of options and is really nicely laid out for you. You can also see on the left (blue) that this is where your slides will appear.

Once you click on any of the given tools, a list of options will pop up. For example the home tool, which is basically a roundup of all the most important tools on PowerPoint.Tools like the Font size, and adding and editing slides. Once you click over any of these tools they will tell you there purpose and how to perform them.

Another tool is the design tab. This is for designing the look of your presentation. It comes with many different designs to make your presentation look unique.

The Transistions tab is for how your slides will transistion into one another. There are many different transitions to suit your needs. Once you click on a transition, it will apply to the given slide.

The Animations tab is how text and images appear onto the screen. There are many different animations to suit your needs. Once you click on a animation, it will apply to the given text or image.
